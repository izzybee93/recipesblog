---
title: Hasselback butternut squash
date: '2025-08-16T15:27:07.289Z'
categories:
  - mains
featured_image: /images/recipes/hasselback-butternut-squash.jpeg
servings: serves 8
ingredients:
  - 1 medium butternut squash, halved and peeled
  - 60ml water
  - '2 tbsp olive oil, plus more to taste'
  - '¼ onion, finely chopped'
  - 2 tbsp dijon mustard
  - 3 tbsp maple syrup
  - 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  - '¼ tsp sea salt, to taste'
  - 'Ground black pepper, to taste'
  - '30g hazelnuts, toasted and roughly chopped'
  - 60g pomegranate seeds
  - '10g chives, thinly sliced'
directions:
  - >-
    Preheat the oven to 220°C.
  - >-
    Place the squash face down on a large baking tray and pour in the water. Bake
    the squash for 20 minutes, until the flesh is softened but not fully cooked. Leave to cool for 10 minutes.
  - >-
    Place one half of the squash face down. Carefully cut very thin slits,
    about 3mm wide, about ¾ of the way through the squash, being careful not to
    cut all the way through. Repeat with the second half.
  - >-
    Line the tray place the squash face down. Brush with olive oil and bake for another 35 minutes, until the softened and
    golden brown on top.
  - >-
    To make the sauce, heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a small saucepan
    over medium heat. Add the onion and cook for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until
    it begins to caramelise. Add the mustard, maple syrup, and apple cider vinegar and whisk to combine.
    Simmer for 3 minutes, until the sauce is slightly reduced and thickened.
    Season with salt and ground black pepper.
  - >-
    Drizzle the warm sauce over the butternut squash, making sure to pour the
    sauce in between the slits. Top with the hazelnuts,
    pomegranate seeds and chives. Serve warm.
