---
title: "Dark rye bread"
date: "2026-03-18"
categories:
  - bread
featured_image: "/images/recipes/dark-rye-bread.jpeg"
servings: "makes 1 loaf"
ingredients:
  - 300g strong white bread flour
  - 180g rye flour
  - 7g instant yeast
  - 8g sea salt
  - 10g cocoa powder
  - 1 tbsp caraway seeds (optional)
  - 40g dark brown sugar
  - 330ml warm water
  - 2 tbsp vegetable oil
directions:
  - 'In a large bowl, mix the bread flour, rye flour, yeast and salt. Sift the cocoa powder into the bowl, add the seeds and mix well.'
  - 'In a measuring jug, mix the water and sugar until dissolved, then stir in the oil. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and mix until a sticky dough forms.'
  - 'Cover with a damp tea towel and leave to rest for 1-2 hours until doubled in size. Perform a stretch and fold with at least 6 folds. Cover and leave to rest for another 2-3 hours. At this stage, either bake immediately or transfer to the fridge for 8-16 hours for flavour development.'
  - 'When ready to bake, if refrigerated, remove the bowl from the fridge for an hour before baking. Preheat the oven to 200°C. Place the lid of the baking dish in the oven to preheat.'
  - 'Shape the dough by folding the sides inwards (about 6 folds) to roughly form a round shape.'
  - 'Remove the lid from the oven, dust with flour and place the dough on top. Cut 4 slashes into the dough, then place the dish on top.'
  - 'Bake for 30 minutes covered, then 10 minutes uncovered at 180°C, until the loaf is well browned and crispy. Transfer to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.'
