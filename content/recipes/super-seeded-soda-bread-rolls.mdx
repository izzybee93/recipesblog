servings: "makes 16"
ingredients:
  - 500g strong bread flour or plain flour
  - 2 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  - 8g sea salt
  - 1 400g can chickpeas
  - 150ml soya milk, plus extra for brushing
  - 2 tbsp olive oil
  - 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  - 30g sunflower seeds
  - 20g pumpkin seeds
  - 20g chia seeds
  - 10g flaxseeds
  - '## Topping'
  - 15g sesame seeds
directions:
  - In a small bowl, add the chia seeds and 4 tbsp water. Set aside for 10 minutes.
  - In a large bowl, mix together the flour, baking powder, salt and seeds.
  - In a blender, add the chickpeas and aquafaba, soya milk, olive oil and apple cider vinegar. Blend until smooth. Add the chickpea mixture and chia seeds to the dry ingredients. Mix until a dough forms, then cover and leave to reset for 10 minutes.
  - Preheat the oven to 180°C. Prepare 2 large baking trays with parchment paper.
  - Divide the dough into 16 equal portions. Shape each portion into round ball, then press to flatten slightly.
  - Transfer to a baking tray. Brush the top of each roll with soya milk. Top with sesame seeds.
  - Bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown. For crusty bread, increase the oven to 190°C and bake for another 5 minutes. Transfer to a wire rack to cool before serving.
